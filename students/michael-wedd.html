Biography

Before getting my B.A. in cultural anthropology from Columbia University, I was entirely self-educated. I grew up as one of eight in a farm house in Philmont, NY, two and a half hours north of New York City. I pursued a career as a classical concert pianist from age nine to 21. After years of performing and teaching, I suffered a performance injury and had to switch life paths dramatically. During my freshman summer, on a 6,000 mile endurance bicycle tour with my younger brother from New York to Alaska and to San Francisco, I decided I would go back to Columbia and major in cultural anthropology, blending my creative past with a more research-driven future where cultural empathy and global leadership were central. In senior year, torn between whether to pursue a Ph.D in anthropology and start a brand strategy business with my elder sister, I met Andrew Yang at Princeton and was compelled to become a VFA fellow to gain new exposure both to a network of young entrepreneurs and to the opportunity to try on new hats. I am thrilled to be moving to Baltimore in August as a Product Manager at Leverege.
